# Lab 2 – Copilot Meeting Scheduling \& Inbox Prioritization

## Objectives

By the end of this lab, participants will be able to:

* Use Microsoft Copilot in Outlook to analyze and prioritize a high volume of emails.
* Identify urgent, high‑impact messages, including those related to compliance risks.
* Extract, organize, and analyze meetings from the Outlook calendar.
* Automatically generate professional email replies, meeting agendas, and schedule meetings using Copilot.

## Context

This lab is based on a realistic **“back from leave with an overloaded inbox”** scenario. Participants must quickly regain control of their inbox and calendar by leveraging Microsoft Copilot to save time, reduce risk, and focus on what matters most.

## Scenario

You are returning from vacation and your Outlook inbox contains many unread emails, complex conversation threads, and multiple meetings already scheduled or still to be organized. Your goals are to:

* Identify what is truly urgent and important
* Quickly understand the context of email conversations
* Efficiently prepare and schedule meetings

Copilot acts as your personal assistant to analyze, summarize, and take action.

## Prerequisites

* A Microsoft 365 work account
* Access to Outlook (Web or Desktop)
* Copilot enabled in Outlook
* An existing set of emails and meetings (real environment or demo tenant)

\---

## Part 1 – Inbox Analysis and Prioritization

1. Open Outlook: https://outlook.office.com/mail/
2. Click the **Copilot** icon in the top‑right corner

### Prompts to use

```text
Prompt 1: What matters most in my inbox today?
```

```text
Prompt 2: Identify urgent and high-impact emails in my inbox, including read ones
```

```text
Prompt 3: Are there any upcoming deadlines I should be aware of?
```

```text
Prompt 4: Summarize all emails from the past 5 days. Highlight key topics, action items, and any urgent matters.
```

```text
Prompt 5: Analyze my inbox and prioritize emails based on urgency, deadlines, client impact, and compliance risk. Provide a ranked list with reasoning.
```

\---

## Part 2 – Outlook Calendar Analysis

### List meetings

```text
Prompt 6: Provide a list of all meetings scheduled in my Outlook calendar for today, including time, duration, participants, and meeting subject
```

```text
Prompt 7: Provide a list of all meetings scheduled in my Outlook calendar for this week, including time, duration, participants, and meeting subject
```

```text
Prompt 8: List all meetings scheduled in my Outlook calendar for this week, grouped by day. Provide the result as a table.
```

```text
Prompt 9: List the meetings scheduled for this week related to the \[X] project including past and future meetings.
```

```text
Prompt 10: List the meetings where \[X] is participating.
```

\---

## Part 3 – Email Thread Analysis

```text
Prompt 11: Retrieve all items related to the \[X] email thread, including emails, attachments, meeting invitations, and follow-up actions. Provide a structured summary including key decisions, open questions, deadlines, and next steps.
```

### Drafting an email with Copilot

```text
Prompt 12: Draft a professional email reply within the \[X] thread to confirm the schedule and my participation. Mention that I am available and fully prepared for the meeting. Thank the organizers for the coordination. Use a clear, confident, and professional tone.
```

Then use **Auto rewrite → Make it longer**, and select **Keep it**.

\---

## Part 4 – Participant Analysis

```text
Prompt 13: Analyze this email and list all participants involved in this conversation. Include the sender, recipients (To), CC, and any other visible participants. For each person, indicate their email address, role in the organization, role in the thread, and summarize their level of involvement.
```

\---

## Part 5 – Meeting Scheduling with Copilot

```text
Prompt 14: Schedule a meeting for next week with all participants in this email thread. Check attendees’ availability and propose the best available time slot. Include a clear meeting subject and a brief agenda based on the context of this conversation.
```

```text
Prompt 15: Based on the context of this meeting and the related email thread, generate a structured meeting agenda. Include a clear objective, key discussion topics, time allocation for each item, and expected outcomes.
```

```text
Prompt 16: Analyze this email thread and schedule a 60-minute follow-up meeting with all relevant participants. Select the earliest available time slot next week based on my calendar and attendees’ availability. Create the meeting and add it directly to my Outlook calendar.
Include in the invitation:
- Professional meeting title
- Meeting objective
- Structured agenda with time allocation
- Decision items
- Expected outcomes
- Identified risks and open questions
- Microsoft Teams meeting link
```
