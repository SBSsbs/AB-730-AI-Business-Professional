# Copilot in Microsoft Teams: Analyzing HR Training Conversations

> ⚠️ \*\*Important note for participants\*\*  
> The prompts provided in this lab are \*\*not perfect by design\*\*. They are starting points.  
> You are expected to \*\*improve, refine, and adapt them\*\* based on:
> - What you learned during this session
> - Your own HR use case
> - The type of conversation you are analyzing
>
> You can apply this lab to \*\*any Microsoft Teams conversation or meeting\*\* you have access to.

\---

## Objectives

The objective of this lab is to help participants understand how **Copilot in Microsoft Teams** can support HR professionals in analyzing conversations related to training sessions and HR use cases.

By the end of this lab, participants will be able to:

* Use Copilot to summarize meetings and channel conversations
* Identify decisions, action items, risks, and open questions from discussions
* Adapt Copilot outputs to different audiences (HR managers, trainers, executives)
* Leverage Copilot to improve follow-up, accountability, and knowledge sharing in HR contexts

\---

## Context

This lab is based on a **Microsoft Teams channel conversation** following a training session on **Copilot use cases in the HR domain**.

The discussion typically includes exchanges between HR stakeholders, trainers, and participants about topics such as:

* HR productivity and automation use cases
* Recruitment, onboarding, and employee experience scenarios
* Questions, feedback, and next steps after the training

The volume and informal nature of these conversations make it difficult to quickly extract key information manually, which makes them an ideal scenario to demonstrate Copilot’s value.

\---

## Scenario

You are an **HR professional or HR program lead** who has access to a Microsoft Teams channel used for a training session on Copilot HR use cases.

After the session:

* Multiple messages have been posted in the channel
* Important decisions, follow-up actions, and concerns are scattered across the conversation
* Some stakeholders did not attend the session and need a clear and structured summary

Your task is to use **Copilot in Microsoft Teams** to analyze a meeting and/or channel conversation, extract insights, and produce clear, structured outputs that can be shared with different audiences.

You are free to choose **any Teams conversation or meeting** available in your environment.

\---

## Prerequisites

Before starting this lab, participants should:

* Have access to **Microsoft Teams with Copilot enabled**
* Be a member of at least one Teams channel containing conversations or meetings
* Have access to a meeting recording, transcript, or channel conversation
* Possess a basic understanding of HR processes (e.g. recruitment, training, employee lifecycle)
* Be familiar with standard Teams features such as meetings, channels, and chat messages

## Copilot for Microsoft Teams – Practice Lab Prompts

This section contains hands-on prompts designed to practice using Copilot in Microsoft Teams.  
Each prompt is accompanied by a short explanation to clarify its intent and expected value.

### Prompt 1: Summarize a meeting

**Prompt:**  
"Summarize the meeting related to HR Copilot use cases in 5 key bullet points. Highlight the main topics discussed."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Helps quickly understand what was discussed during the meeting and ensures alignment for people who could not attend.

\---

### Prompt 2: Decisions and actions

**Prompt:**  
"Identify all decisions made during this meeting and list the related action items."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Demonstrates how Copilot can extract decisions and transform discussions into actionable outcomes.

\---

### Prompt 3: Missed meeting recap

**Prompt:**  
"I could not attend this meeting. Provide a clear summary focused on outcomes and next steps."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Supports asynchronous work and reduces meeting overload.

\---

### Prompt 4: Open questions

**Prompt:**  
"What questions or topics were left unresolved at the end of this meeting or conversation?"

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Helps teams track follow-ups and unfinished discussions.

\---

### Prompt 5: Disagreements or differing viewpoints

**Prompt:**  
"Are there any disagreements or conflicting viewpoints expressed in this discussion?"

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Highlights areas of misalignment that may require follow-up or clarification.

\---

### Prompt 6: Implicit action items

**Prompt:**  
"Identify any implicit requests or action items mentioned in this chat or meeting."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Demonstrates Copilot’s ability to reason beyond explicit statements.

\---

### Prompt 7: Shared resources

**Prompt:**  
"Provide a list of all documents, links, or resources shared during this session."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Improves clarity and ensures important resources are not lost in the conversation.

\---

### Prompt 8: Shareable meeting notes

**Prompt:**  
"Turn this meeting or conversation into a structured set of notes that can be shared with the team."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Demonstrates Copilot’s ability to transform unstructured content into reusable documentation.

\---

### Prompt 9: Action plan

**Prompt:**  
"Based on this meeting or conversation, create a clear action plan in a table format."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Shows how Copilot can produce structured, execution-oriented outputs.

\---

### Prompt 10: Commitment tracking

**Prompt:**  
"List the commitments made by each participant during this meeting or conversation."

**Why this prompt matters:**  
Supports accountability and follow-up.

\---
